Privacy Policy for Go345

Effective date: August 19, 2025

This page explains how the Go345 app ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use Go345 for iOS.

Summary: Go345 shows ads using Google Mobile Ads (AdMob). If you deny tracking, the app requests non‑personalized ads. Gameplay remains available regardless of your choice.

1\) Who we are

  • App: Go345 (iOS)
  • Developer: MozSoft
  • Contact: use the "App Support" link on the App Store product page to reach the developer

2\) Scope

This policy applies to the Go345 iOS app and related in‑app features, support, and communications.

3\) Information we collect

Information you provide directly:

  • Support communications you send to the developer via the App Store "App Support" link or email, including any information you choose to share.

Information collected automatically by the app or its SDKs:

  • Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) SDK: may collect device identifiers (for example, Apple’s IDFA when permission is granted), IP address, app instance identifiers, approximate location inferred from IP, device and OS information, ad interactions, and diagnostics for ads delivery and measurement.
  • Local app data: Go345 stores gameplay preferences and high scores on your device using iOS storage (for example, UserDefaults). This data is not transmitted to developer‑controlled servers.
  • Multiplayer (peer‑to‑peer): if you use local multiplayer, gameplay messages are exchanged directly between devices using Apple’s MultipeerConnectivity and are not sent to developer‑controlled servers.

Learn more about Google’s practices:

  • https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads
  • https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • https://support.google.com/admob/answer/6128543

4\) How we use information

  • Operate and improve the app and its features.
  • Display advertising and measure ad performance.
  • Prevent fraud, secure, debug, and ensure proper functioning.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce terms.

5\) Advertising, tracking, and choices \(AdMob\)

  • Apple AppTrackingTransparency \(ATT\): The app may request permission to "Allow Tracking".
  • If you allow tracking: AdMob may access the IDFA and combine data across apps and sites to deliver personalized ads and measure performance.
  • If you deny tracking: the app requests non‑personalized ads by setting the parameter npa=1 on every ad request. Ads may still appear using contextual signals without using the IDFA.
  • Feature access: all gameplay features remain available regardless of your tracking choice.
  • Regional consent \(EEA/UK\): the app follows Google’s EU User Consent Policy and honors regional consent requirements where applicable.

6\) Legal bases for processing \(EEA/UK\)

  • Consent: personalized advertising, access to IDFA, and any regionally required consents.
  • Legitimate interests: app security, non‑personalized advertising, basic diagnostics, and service operation.
  • Contract: to provide the app you request.

7\) Sharing of information

  • Service providers/partners: Google \(AdMob\) processes data for advertising as described in Google’s policies.
  • Legal and compliance: information may be disclosed to comply with law, enforce terms, or protect rights, safety, and security.
  • The developer does not sell personal information.

8\) Data retention

The app retains only what is necessary for the purposes described. Local gameplay data remains on your device unless you delete the app or reset data. AdMob sets its own retention periods per Google’s policies.

9\) Children’s privacy

Go345 is not directed to children under 13 \(or the relevant age in your jurisdiction\). The developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child provided personal information, use the App Store "App Support" link to contact the developer and request deletion.

10\) Your rights and controls

  • EEA/UK: rights to access, rectify, erase, restrict, portability, and object; withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing.
  • California \(CCPA/CPRA\): rights to know, delete, correct, and opt‑out of sharing for cross‑context behavioral advertising. The developer does not sell personal information.
  • iOS settings: manage tracking under Settings \> Privacy & Security \> Tracking. You can also reset or limit the advertising identifier in iOS settings \(varies by version\).

For ad‑related data that Google processes, see Google’s privacy policy and controls. For app‑specific requests, use the "App Support" link on the App Store product page.

11\) International transfers

Data may be processed in countries other than your own. Where required, appropriate safeguards \(for example, standard contractual clauses\) are used by providers such as Google.

12\) Security

The app uses reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

13\) Third‑party links

The app may link to third‑party sites or services not controlled by the developer. Their privacy practices apply.

14\) Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated periodically. The "Effective date" will be revised and, where required, additional notice will be provided.

15\) App Store Connect disclosures \(summary\)

  • Data Used to Track You: Identifiers \(IDFA\) if you allow tracking.
  • Data Linked to You: Identifiers, usage data, diagnostics as applicable via AdMob for advertising and measurement.
  • Purposes: third‑party advertising, analytics/diagnostics, and app functionality.
